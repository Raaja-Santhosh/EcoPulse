The Basics

What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total volume of greenhouse gases (including carbon dioxide and methane) generated by our collective actions. It acts as an ecological shadow—measuring how our travel, diet, and utility usage shape the atmosphere.

Measured in metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent (t CO₂e/yr), understanding this baseline is the first step toward environmental balance.

The Contributors

Where does it come from?

Daily human activities are the direct cause of rising atmospheric carbon. Four key sectors drive our footprints:

Energy & Utility (31%)

Coal and natural gas burned to power home heating, cooling, and electronic devices.

Transportation (15%)

Single-occupancy petrol vehicles and global flights releasing raw greenhouse exhausts.

Diet & Food (26%)

Mass livestock farming (emitting methane) and long-distance food transportation.

Waste & Shopping (28%)

Industrial manufacturing of consumer goods and landfills emitting methane from organic waste.

Core Concept

What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint represents the total greenhouse gas emissions (including carbon dioxide and methane) caused directly and indirectly by an individual, event, organization, or product.

It acts as your atmospheric shadow. Every flight taken, light switched on, and meal consumed contributes to this collective impact, measured in metric tons of CO₂ equivalent (t CO₂e) per year.
